Overview of this project

Asteroids, commonly known as minor planets, are remnants from the early formation of our solar system. They range in size from tiny pebbles to hundreds of kilometers in diameter. Some asteroids follow orbits that bring them close to Earth, and these Near-Earth Objects (NEOs) pose potential collision risks.

The possibility of an asteroid impact has long been a concern for scientists, policymakers, and the general public. Throughout history, large asteroid collisions have been linked to significant environmental changes and mass extinctions. Understanding their behavior and predicting their trajectories is critical for planetary defense.

Current asteroid tracking methods use telescopes and radar technology to monitor their positions and estimate their future locations. However, due to the vast number of asteroids and the complexities of gravitational influences, traditional tracking methods face limitations. This is where artificial intelligence and machine learning come into play, offering enhanced precision and predictive modeling capabilities.

Despite advancements, challenges remain in identifying and categorizing asteroids efficiently. Many NEOs are discovered only after they have passed Earth. Developing advanced analytical tools that can process astronomical data quickly and accurately will be crucial in mitigating potential threats.

Ongoing research focuses on improving asteroid classification, refining impact probability calculations, and designing deflection strategies. Future missions, such as NASA’s DART (Double Asteroid Redirection Test), aim to test technologies that could alter an asteroid’s trajectory, providing insights into planetary defense techniques.
